Operational Technology and Industrial Control Systems

Organisations are facing increasing operational risks as cyber threat actors shift focus from the Information Technology (IT) networks that run business systems to the OT networks that control industrial operations. This can lead to significant operational disruption and increased safety risks to people and the environment. Most organizations do not know where to go to find the expertise required to develop and implement cybersecurity programs for ICS.

Hamilton typically works directly with operations personnel to support their operational cybersecurity needs. In many cases, ABS Group is assisting operations personnel to develop a business case for OT cybersecurity services in defense of IT services that are being pressed on them from the IT side of the organization. After all, there is a high potential for threats being introduced to OT networks born out of IT-OT convergence, or IT networks being connected to OT networks.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  • What is ICS cyber security?

    "ICS" stands for Industrial Control System. Industrial Control System cybersecurity is any form of advanced cybersecurity protection specifically designed for computerized technologies that support operational environments versus IT-only environments. Industrial Control System examples include but are not limited to the following:


    • Human Machine Interfaces (HMI)
    • Engineering Workstations (EWS)
    • Programmable Logic Controllers (PLC)
    • Remote Terminal Units (RTU)
    • Distributed Control Systems (DCS)
  • What is OT security?

    "OT" stands for Operational Technology. Operational Technology security is often used synonymously with Industrial Control System cybersecurity to distinguish computer security practices from those typically found in IT environments. Operational Technology security more generically refers to the cybersecurity practices employed by industrial control systems operations. While most organizations have IT support and IT cybersecurity programs, many with industrial controls do not have an equivalent support team in operations. (There is a clear distinction between Operational Technology support and IT support.) This leaves large cybersecurity gaps and exposes the Industrial Control System that is running operations.

  • Why does my business need industrial network cybersecurity?

    Organizations with Operational Technology and a networked Industrial Control System are vulnerable to cybersecurity attacks resulting in loss of production, impact to the environment or loss of human life. For example, elevators are controlled by computerized technologies called PLC. If an elevator's PLC is compromised, a threat actor could control whether that elevator full of people goes up or down. Industrial network security helps to mitigate risks and avoid potential disruptions by providing a monitoring capability to identify threats before the attack occurs.

  • What are best practices for ICS cybersecurity?

    There are several best practices for Industrial Control System cybersecurity. They include, but are not limited to:

    • Industrial control network monitoring
    • Threat detection and incident response
    • Vulnerability management automation
    • Access controls
    • Consolidation and centralization of user account management
    • Training (i.e., establishing a deeper understanding of Industrial Control System security)
  • I have ICS/OT security measures in place. Do I still need additional protection?

    While many organizations may already have their Industrial Control System and Operational Technology security covered, these systems, tools and practices have a shelf life. Cyber threats are constantly evolving, and you should too. In order to adhere to top Operational Technology and Industrial Control System security standards, it is crucial to upgrade your Operational Technology and Industrial Control System cybersecurity protection and protocols as often as possible. Employing a cybersecurity technique called "Cartography", ABS Group's security engineers review your existing cybersecurity controls and practices, compare the "as-is" security environment with the much-needed regulatory and best-practice cybersecurity controls and provide you with an optimistic "to-be" road map, helping you to manage costs against the overall benefit of your cybersecurity updates.
